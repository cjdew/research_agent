## A. Situation Assessment

### What Is Ready to Act On

1. **The four-lane model is validated and operational.** Systems/Compliance, Comms/Executive Flow, Ministry Owner, and Chris Only are structurally sound. No redesign needed. Proceed with implementation. *[Organizational Structure]*

2. **The ownership taxonomy (Own/Support/Prep/Track/Escalate) is sound** but needs one critical enhancement: splitting "Own" into Own-Execute and Own-Decide for every high-risk task. This is the single most important structural fix. *[Decision-Rights Clarity]*

3. **20-25 tasks per lane are genuinely Gate 0 (immediate handoff).** These require no SOP, no special training, and no decision-authority ambiguity. They can move on day one. *[Delegation]*

4. **The two-admin specialized model is the right staffing structure for this stage.** Not one generalist, not a Chief of Staff. Two people with different skill profiles covering different lanes. *[Role Design]*

### What Needs Decisions First

1. **Financial thresholds.** The framework references escalation at dollar amounts ($500 purchase requests, $5,000 donor communications). Chris must set specific thresholds before delegation levels can be operationalized. *[Decision-Rights Clarity]*
   - **Assumed defaults used in this package:** Purchase requests escalate at $500. Donor personalized communications escalate at $5,000. Budget anomalies escalate at 5% variance or $1,000 unexpected. Adjust these before rollout.

2. **Board alignment.** The board may have expectations about admin access to financial data, HR processes, and benevolence decisions. Chris should brief the board on the delegation model before full implementation. *[Organizational Structure]*

3. **Ministry owner readiness.** Not all ministry owners are at the same maturity level. Chris must assess each one before re-homing tasks. The Ministry Owner Test (Section H) provides the structure. *[Leader Readiness]*

4. **Technology platform.** Tracking 130+ tasks across readiness gates, delegation levels, and escalation triggers manually will become its own bottleneck. A task management tool (even a well-structured spreadsheet) must be in place by Week 1. *[Management Cadence]*

### What Needs More Information

1. **Who manages the admins day-to-day?** Chris is assumed as direct supervisor for both. With 5-7 ministry owner reports plus two admins, effective span of control is 7-9. This is viable for months 1-6 but may require structural adjustment if one admin develops Chief of Staff capacity. *[Organizational Structure]*

2. **What happens when ministry owners refuse to own?** The accountability framework provides structure, but some ministry leaders may lack operational maturity. This may require role redesign or personnel decisions that go beyond delegation. *[Leader Readiness]*

3. **Existing software integrations.** How Planning Center, email platforms, and church management software connect to the delegation framework affects what can be automated versus manually delegated. *[Delegation]*

| L4 | Decide and Inform | Makes the call, tells Chris afterward | Reviews after the fact |

## C. Role Definitions

### 1. Systems / Compliance Admin

**Core identity:** Process architect and compliance guardian. Makes sure things are done right, on time, and documented.

**Responsibilities:**
- Financial admin: AP, invoices, reimbursements, purchase requests under threshold
- Database and Planning Center hygiene
- Background check process management (ordering, tracking -- NOT adjudication)
- Onboarding/offboarding mechanics
- HR record maintenance (restricted access, under Chris's authority)
- Knowledge base, SOPs, document management
- Access management, license tracking, audit prep
- Compliance monitoring and policy acknowledgment tracking
- Facilities admin, booking, vendor coordination
- Dashboard and reporting support
- Annual compliance calendar management

**Decision authority:**
- L4-L5 on routine process tasks (supply ordering, room booking, document filing)
- L3 on moderate-judgment tasks (vendor quotes, database decisions, contractor coordination)
- L1-L2 on personnel records, compliance findings, expenditures above threshold

## E. Escalation Map

### Escalation Tiers

**Tier 1: Immediate (call/text Chris within 1 hour)**
- Any safety threat to a person (self-harm, abuse, violence)
- Any security breach (physical or digital)
- Any media inquiry or public crisis
- Any legal threat or government contact
- Any contested termination or potential legal exposure
- Any Tier 3 prayer request (crisis-level content)

**Tier 2: Same Business Day (message Chris by end of day)**
- Any adverse background check finding
- Any HR complaint, discipline situation, or personnel conflict
- Any communication involving board members or elders
- Any donor communication above $5,000 threshold
- Any unbudgeted expenditure above $500
- Any compliance violation discovered
- Any offboarding with contested circumstances

**Tier 3: Next Scheduled Check-in (surface at weekly 1-on-1)**
- Budget anomaly >5% variance or >$1,000
- Recurring process failure (3+ occurrences)
- Ministry owner non-compliance with accountability cadence
- Vendor scope or cost change
- Non-urgent policy question
- Tone/voice question on communications
- Access exception request

**Tier 4: Informational Only (include in weekly summary)**
- Routine task completion metrics
- Standard prayer request routing
- Completed background checks (clean)
- Vendor coordination updates
- Calendar changes within normal parameters
- Dashboard health indicators

### What Does NOT Need Escalation

These items should be handled at the admin level without involving Chris. If Chris is being asked about these, reverse delegation is occurring:

- Routing a standard email to the right person
- Scheduling a standard meeting
- Processing a purchase request under $500
- Filing a document
- Sending a templated communication
- Ordering standard supplies
- Updating a database record per SOP
- Booking a room per standard policy
- Sending a KPI collection reminder
- Running a background check (ordering it; not reviewing results)
- Updating the org chart per Chris's prior direction
- Logging an invoice per established coding
- Sending a volunteer paperwork reminder

### Escalation Routing

```
WHO ESCALATES TO WHOM

Systems Admin → Chris
  Route: Slack message (Tier 3-4) | Text/call (Tier 1-2)
  Backup if Chris unavailable (Tier 1): Senior Pastor

Comms Admin → Chris
  Route: Slack message (Tier 3-4) | Text/call (Tier 1-2)
  Backup if Chris unavailable (Tier 1): Senior Pastor

Ministry Owner → Chris
  Route: Weekly huddle (Tier 3-4) | Slack message (Tier 2) | Text/call (Tier 1)
  Backup if Chris unavailable (Tier 1): Senior Pastor

Cross-Lane Handoff:
  Comms Admin discovers pastoral issue → Routes to Pastoral Staff + flags Chris
  Sys Admin discovers personnel issue → Flags Chris directly (does not route to Comms)
  Ministry Owner needs admin support → Requests through established channel, not ad hoc
```

### Response Time Expectations

| Tier | Chris Response Window | Admin Action While Waiting |
|------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Tier 1 (Immediate) | 1 hour | Secure the situation; do not act on the decision; contact backup if no response |
| Tier 2 (Same Day) | By end of business | Document the situation; hold any related actions; do not guess |
| Tier 3 (Check-in) | At next scheduled 1-on-1 | Note it for discussion; proceed on routine elements; hold judgment elements |
| Tier 4 (Informational) | No response required | Include in weekly summary; proceed as normal |

## H. Ministry Owner Accountability Plan

### What Ministry Owners Receive

**From Chris:**
- Clear outcome definitions for every re-homed task (what success looks like)
- Authority to make decisions within their ministry domain (budget limits, staffing decisions within approved headcount, scheduling)
- Regular 1-on-1 coaching (monthly, 45-60 min)
- Weekly team huddle (15 min) for cross-functional coordination
- Quarterly ministry review (90 min) for strategic assessment

**From Admin Support:**
- Templates for KPIs, debriefs, volunteer tracking
- Automated reminders for deadlines and reporting
- Data reports and dashboards for their ministry area
- Communication execution (sending approved messages)
- Logistics coordination (scheduling, materials, room booking)
- Paperwork tracking (volunteer screening status, training completion)

**What they do NOT receive:**
- An admin who attends their team meetings
- An admin who makes ministry decisions on their behalf
- An admin who is their personal assistant
- Permission to push ownership back to admin for items in the Ministry Owner lane

### Training and Support

**Week 3-4: Orientation**
- 30-minute individual meeting with Chris: "Here is what you now own, why, and what support looks like"
- Written outcome definitions for Phase 1 tasks
- Introduction to admin support model (what to request, how, and what is out of scope)
- Template walkthrough for KPI submission and event after-action

**Week 5-8: Guided Practice**
- First KPI submission with Chris feedback
- First event debrief using template with Chris observation
- Weekly huddle includes "ministry owner check-in" standing item
- Chris coaches in 1-on-1s: "How is ownership of X going? What support do you need?"

**Week 9-12: Independent Ownership**
- Ministry owners submitting KPIs without prompting
- Ministry owners leading debriefs independently
- Ministry owners making volunteer decisions within their domain
- Chris reviews outcomes, not process

### Accountability Cadence

| Cadence | Purpose | Participants | Duration |
|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Weekly team huddle | Status, blockers, escalations, cross-functional coordination | Chris + all ministry owners | 15 min |
| Monthly 1-on-1 | KPI review, development conversation, outcome assessment, coaching | Chris + individual ministry owner | 45-60 min |
| Quarterly ministry review | Strategic assessment, resource planning, outcome evaluation, goal setting | Chris + ministry owner + relevant admin (data only) | 90 min |

### What Happens When Ministry Owners Fail to Own

**Level 1: Coaching conversation (first occurrence)**
- Chris addresses directly in 1-on-1: "I notice [task] is being routed to [admin]. That outcome belongs to you. What support do you need to own it?"
- Identify root cause: capacity, competence, authority, or willingness
- Agree on specific corrective action and timeline

**Level 2: Structured support (repeated pattern within 30 days)**
- Chris and ministry owner co-create a 30-day ownership plan
- Specific weekly milestones for the task in question
- Admin support explicitly defined and bounded
- Weekly check-in on progress

**Level 3: Role assessment (persistent failure after 60 days)**
- Chris evaluates whether the issue is:
  - Capacity (too much on the plate -- reduce scope)
  - Competence (skill gap -- provide training or restructure)
  - Authority (insufficient decision rights -- expand or clarify)
  - Willingness (resistance to operational ownership -- direct conversation about role fit)
- If willingness: direct conversation that operational ownership is a non-negotiable part of the role
- If persistent: role redesign or personnel decision (Chris Only)

### How Admin Support Works Without Creating Dependency

**The Anti-Dependency Rule:**
Admin support for ministry owners follows a strict boundary: admins handle logistics, data, templates, reminders, and communication execution. Ministry owners handle strategy, decisions, team leadership, interpretation, and outcomes.

**Practical guardrails:**
1. Admins never attend ministry team meetings as the operational representative
2. Admins never make decisions about volunteers, program design, or team structure
3. When a ministry owner says "just have the admin handle it" for a Ministry Owner task, the admin responds: "I can prepare [specific logistics item] for you, but you will need to decide [specific ownership item]"
4. If the pattern persists (3+ redirects in a month), the admin flags it for Chris
5. Chris addresses it in the next 1-on-1

### Signals to Watch For

**Healthy signals:**
- Escalation volume decreasing week over week
- Admins making more L4 decisions confidently
- Ministry owners submitting KPIs without reminders
- Chris's calendar has more strategic blocks, fewer operational fires
- SOPs being followed consistently
- New situations handled by referencing existing frameworks

**Unhealthy signals:**
- Escalation volume increasing or flat after Week 4
- Admins asking permission for tasks already at L4-L5
- Ministry owners copying admin on decisions that are theirs to make
- Chris re-doing work that was delegated
- SOPs being ignored or worked around
- Same questions coming up repeatedly

### How to Catch Reverse Delegation

Ask these questions in every admin 1-on-1:

1. "What decisions did you make this week without involving me?"
   - If the answer is "none" after Week 4, reverse delegation is occurring
2. "What came back to me this week that you could have handled?"
   - Honest self-assessment; if admin cannot identify anything, probe deeper
3. "What did I grab back that I should have left with you?"
   - Chris's own habits are the #1 driver of reverse delegation
4. "Where did you feel uncertain this week?"
   - Uncertainty is normal; the question surfaces training or authority gaps before they become patterns
5. "What would you have done differently if I were not available?"
   - Builds autonomous judgment; reveals whether the admin is thinking independently

### How to Assess Whether Delegation Is Working

**Monthly self-assessment (Chris):**
1. How many hours this month did I spend on tasks that are in someone else's lane? (Target: <10% of total)
2. How many decisions did I make that were below my threshold? (Target: decreasing monthly)
3. Which ministry owners sought me out for decisions that were theirs to make? (Track names and frequency)
4. Did any dropped ball this month result from delegation? (If yes, was the root cause training, authority, or monitoring?)
5. Am I spending more time on strategy, people development, and board relations than I was 30 days ago?

## J. Practical Tools

### Tool 1: Delegation Decision Filter

Use this step-by-step filter when any new task or request arrives.

```
DELEGATION DECISION FILTER
===========================

STEP 1: Does this require MY unique judgment, relationships, or authority?
  - Personnel decisions (hire, fire, discipline, compensation) → CHRIS ONLY
  - Board/elder relationships or communication → CHRIS ONLY
  - Mission/vision/values decisions → CHRIS ONLY
  - Legal exposure decisions → CHRIS ONLY
  - Speaking for the senior pastor → CHRIS ONLY
  YES to any → Do it yourself. Stop here.
  NO → Continue to Step 2.

STEP 2: Does this involve sensitive or confidential information?
  - Prayer request with crisis content (Tier 3) → CHRIS ONLY (after admin routes)
  - Personnel records, discipline, complaints → Process to admin; judgment to CHRIS
  - Donor data above threshold → CHRIS reviews
  - Background check adverse findings → CHRIS decides
  YES → Can the task be split into PROCESS (delegable) and JUDGMENT (Chris)?
    YES → Delegate the process part. Keep the judgment part. Define the split explicitly.
    NO → CHRIS ONLY for now. Revisit when admin reaches Gate 4.
  NO → Continue to Step 3.

STEP 3: Is this a recurring task?
  YES → Does an SOP exist?
    YES → DELEGATE to appropriate lane at appropriate level. Go to Step 4.
    NO → Create the SOP first (delegate SOP drafting at L2). Then delegate.
  NO → Is this a one-time task?
    YES → Handle it once. If it recurs within 30 days, create SOP.
    NO → Continue to Step 4.

STEP 4: Which lane does this belong to?
  - Records, data, compliance, backend process, finance → SYSTEMS/COMPLIANCE ADMIN
  - Communication, scheduling, information flow, executive support → COMMS/EXECUTIVE FLOW ADMIN
  - Ministry results, team leadership, volunteer management → MINISTRY OWNER
  - Unclear → Assign to the admin whose skill profile best fits. Review lane in next 1-on-1.

STEP 5: What delegation level is appropriate?
  - Admin is new to this task (Gate 0-1) → L1-L2 (execute as directed / research and report)
  - Admin has done this before with acceptable quality (Gate 2) → L3 (recommend and wait)
  - Admin has proven track record on this task (Gate 3) → L4 (decide and inform)
  - Admin is trusted autonomous (Gate 4) → L5 (decide and act)

STEP 6: What are the escalation triggers?
  Define specifically: "Escalate to me if _______________."
  If you cannot define specific triggers, you are not ready to delegate this task.

STEP 7: Should this task be ELIMINATED or AUTOMATED instead of delegated?
  - Does this task produce value proportional to the time it takes?
  - Could a system, automation, or template replace a person doing this?
  - If eliminated, what breaks? If nothing breaks, eliminate it.
```

## K. Risk Controls

### 1. Confidential Personnel Matters

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Sys Admin handles process mechanics (scheduling, filing, tracking). Chris handles all substantive decisions and conversations |
| **Who sees data** | Chris + Sys Admin only. Sys Admin access restricted to assigned files. No ministry owner or general staff access |
| **Escalation rule** | Any HR complaint, discipline document, or termination-related matter escalates to Chris same business day |
| **Documentation requirement** | All personnel documents stored in restricted-access location. Marked "CONFIDENTIAL - RESTRICTED DISTRIBUTION." Never in shared drives |
| **Audit frequency** | Quarterly access audit by Chris. Verify only authorized personnel have access |
| **Safeguard** | Confidentiality agreement signed before any access. Admin never communicates discipline, termination, or compensation decisions to staff. That is always Chris |

### 2. Prayer Request Handling

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Comms Admin triages and routes per 3-tier SOP. Pastoral staff handles Tier 2-3. Chris handles Tier 3 oversight |
| **Who sees data** | Comms Admin and pastoral staff only. Not visible to general volunteers or other staff |
| **Escalation rule** | Tier 1 (standard): Route to prayer team, no escalation. Tier 2 (sensitive): Route to pastoral staff with "confidential" flag. Tier 3 (crisis): Immediate escalation to pastoral staff AND Chris |
| **Documentation requirement** | Written consent required before sharing any health information publicly. Prayer request data not stored in general databases |
| **Audit frequency** | Monthly review of routing decisions by Chris (sample 10-15 requests) |
| **Safeguard** | Clergy-penitent privilege does NOT extend to lay staff. Admin must understand they have no legal privilege. SOP must be in place before any delegation. Training on recognizing Tier 2-3 indicators required |

### 3. Benevolence Decisions

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Sys Admin handles intake and documentation. Pastoral staff/benevolence committee assesses need. Chris approves above policy threshold. Finance disburses |
| **Who sees data** | Benevolence committee, pastoral staff, Chris, finance (disbursement only). Benevolence decisions never linked to giving history |
| **Escalation rule** | Any request above policy committee authority escalates to Chris. Any recurring requests from same individual flagged to Chris. Any request from staff member escalates to Chris |
| **Documentation requirement** | Every request documented regardless of outcome. All payments direct to vendors -- no cash. Separation of duties enforced: evaluator, approver, and disburser are different people |
| **Audit frequency** | Quarterly review of all benevolence disbursements by Chris. Annual review by board or designated committee |
| **Safeguard** | Written policy required. Policy reviewed annually. Admin never makes approval decisions. Disbursement records kept separately from general financial records |

### 4. Volunteer Screening / Background Checks

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Sys Admin orders checks, tracks completion status, manages renewal calendar. Chris adjudicates any adverse findings |
| **Who sees data** | Chris sees full results. Sys Admin sees completion status only (cleared / pending / flagged). Ministry owners see cleared/not-cleared status only -- no details |
| **Escalation rule** | Any adverse or ambiguous finding escalates to Chris within 24 hours. Any expired check flagged to ministry owner (do not deploy) and Chris |
| **Documentation requirement** | Screening records stored separately from general volunteer files. Restricted access. Renewal calendar automated with 60-day advance reminders |
| **Audit frequency** | Monthly check of renewal calendar by Sys Admin. Quarterly compliance review by Chris. Annual audit of all active volunteer screening status |
| **Safeguard** | Background checks include criminal registry, sex offender registry, and child abuse registry. Two-person rule (no adult alone with a child) tracked by Sys Admin, implemented by ministry owners. Screening renewed every 3 years minimum |

### 5. Donor Communications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Comms Admin executes templated thank-you communications. Chris reviews/personalizes communications to donors above $5,000 threshold. Chris owns donor relationship strategy |
| **Who sees data** | Giving data restricted to: Chris, finance staff, designated admin with confidentiality agreement. NOT visible to ministry leaders or general staff |
| **Escalation rule** | Any personalized communication to donor >$5,000 requires Chris review before sending. Any donor complaint or concern escalates to Chris same day. Any communication that could be interpreted as a pledge solicitation escalates to Chris |
| **Documentation requirement** | All donor communications logged. Giving data access log maintained. Any verbal commitments from donors documented and shared with Chris |
| **Audit frequency** | Monthly review of donor communication log by Chris. Annual giving data access audit |
| **Safeguard** | Board members have fiduciary confidentiality obligations around donor data. Staff confidentiality agreement covers donor information specifically. Never share individual giving data in staff meetings or public settings |

### 6. Board Packet Preparation

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Who handles** | Comms Admin assembles materials per checklist. Sys Admin prepares data sections. Chris reviews ALL content before distribution to board |
| **Who sees data** | Chris and designated admins (assembly only). Board members (distribution). General staff does NOT see board packets |
| **Escalation rule** | Any data anomaly or sensitive item discovered during assembly escalates to Chris before inclusion. Any board member request for additional information routed to Chris |
| **Documentation requirement** | Board packet checklist completed for every meeting. Distribution list confirmed. Draft reviewed and approved by Chris before any distribution |
| **Audit frequency** | Every board meeting cycle (review checklist completion and distribution accuracy) |
| **Safeguard** | Board packets stored in restricted-access location. Not in general shared drives. Admin assembles; Chris frames narrative and approves. No admin interpretation of data for board consumption without Chris review |

### 7. HR Records

| Element | Specification |

| **Safeguard** | Premature admin involvement in morale signals risks misinterpretation and trust erosion. This function is in Section 6 (Not Yet) for a reason. Do not activate early regardless of how helpful it seems |

## L. What to Watch -- Early Warning Indicators

### Delegation Is Failing

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Tasks returning to Chris at increasing rate | Weekly reverse-delegation audit (Tool 5) shows upward trend after Week 4 | Diagnose root cause per task: training, authority, confidence, or Chris's habit. Address the specific gap |
| Admin quality declining on previously stable tasks | Errors appearing on tasks that were running smoothly | Check for overload (too many tasks added too fast). Reduce scope if needed. Revisit absorption rate |
| Admins not advancing through readiness gates | Same tasks still at Gate 1 after 30 days | Determine whether the issue is training (provide more), confidence (coach more), or competence (wrong person for the task) |
| Chris still making >5 routine decisions per day after Week 4 | Friday self-audit question: "How many decisions did I make this week that were below my threshold?" | Review delegation levels. Advance L3 tasks to L4 where quality supports it. Practice "What do you recommend?" coaching |
| Delegated tasks are being done but outcomes are poor | Output review in 1-on-1s shows technically complete but low-quality work | Clarify "what good looks like." Share examples. Increase check-in cadence temporarily. Determine if SOP is inadequate |

### Reverse Delegation Is Happening

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Admin asks "quick questions" on tasks at L4-L5 | Count questions per week that should not require Chris's input | Respond with coaching: "What would you do if I were unavailable?" Do not answer the question directly |
| Same task returns 3+ times | Reverse-delegation audit pattern check | Root cause analysis. If training: retrain. If authority: expand. If confidence: coach. If Chris's habit: Chris must let go |
| Chris is solving problems instead of approving solutions | Self-assessment: "Did I solve or approve this week?" | Discipline of asking "What do you recommend?" before giving direction |
| Admin says "I wanted to check with you before..." on routine items | 1-on-1 observation | Affirm the admin's authority: "You have the authority to handle this. I trust your judgment. Tell me what you decided" |
| Chris feels indispensable on routine operations | Friday self-audit: "What would break if I took a week off?" | If more than Chris Only items would break, reverse delegation has occurred. Map the gaps and re-delegate |

### Admin Roles Are Becoming Bottlenecks

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Admin consistently behind on task completion | Weekly task tracker shows missed deadlines or delayed outputs | Check capacity: are they overloaded? Reduce scope, defer lower-priority tasks, or identify tasks to automate/eliminate |
| Other staff bypassing admin to go directly to Chris | Staff members emailing Chris directly on admin-lane items | Redirect: "Please route this through [Admin Name]. They handle this." Consistent enforcement |
| Admin becoming a gatekeeper who blocks access | Staff complaints about inability to reach Chris or get decisions | Review calendar protection rules. Ensure admin is not over-filtering. Adjust escalation tiers if needed |
| Admin developing decision authority beyond their role | Admin making calls that should be L2-L3 at L4-L5 | Review specific decisions. If good calls: advance their delegation level. If bad calls: pull back authority and retrain |
| One admin significantly more loaded than the other | Task tracker shows imbalanced workload | Rebalance tasks between lanes. Review if any tasks are in the wrong lane |

### Ministry Owners Are Not Absorbing Ownership

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| KPIs consistently late or missing | KPI collection tracking shows gaps | Level 1 coaching conversation. If persistent: Level 2 structured support plan |
| Ministry owner copies admin on decisions that are theirs | Email/message patterns show admin included on ownership items | Direct conversation: "This decision is yours. What support do you need to make it without routing through admin?" |
| Admin reports ministry owner saying "just handle it" | Admin flags in 1-on-1 (per anti-dependency guardrail) | Chris addresses directly: "I understand [Admin] was asked to handle [task]. That outcome belongs to you" |
| Volunteer complaints going unaddressed in ministry area | Volunteer feedback or exit data shows care gaps | Quarterly ministry owner scorecard (Tool 6) reveals the gap. Coaching or Level 2 intervention |
| Ministry area quality declining despite admin support | Outcome metrics trending negatively | Determine if the issue is capacity (reduce scope), competence (train), authority (expand), or willingness (direct conversation about role expectations) |

### Chris Is Still the Default Decision-Maker

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Chris's calendar is full of operational meetings | Calendar audit: what percentage of meetings are strategic vs. operational? | Delegate operational meeting attendance to admins or ministry owners. Send prep briefs, not Chris |
| Staff default to asking Chris rather than checking SOP | Frequency of "Chris, can I..." questions on SOP-covered topics | Redirect to SOP. If SOP does not exist, create it. If SOP exists and is being ignored, enforce |
| Board packet prep consumes multiple days of Chris's time | Time tracking on board cycle shows Chris doing assembly, not just review | Accelerate board packet assembly delegation. Admin preps; Chris does 1-hour review, not multi-day assembly |
| Chris attends meetings to "make sure it goes right" | Self-audit: which meetings am I attending that I am not needed in? | Test: skip one meeting per week that feels necessary but is not Chris Only. See if it runs without you |

### SOPs Are Not Being Followed

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Same errors recurring despite SOP existence | Error patterns on SOP-covered tasks | Determine if admin is not reading the SOP, SOP is inadequate, or SOP does not match reality. Fix the root cause |
| Admins developing personal workarounds | Process observation shows deviation from documented SOP | Ask why. If the workaround is better: update the SOP. If it is not: retrain and enforce |
| New situations generate confusion despite framework | Admin uncertainty on situations the framework should cover | SOP gap: the situation is not addressed. Add it. Or: admin has not internalized the framework. Coach |
| SOP development has stalled | SOP pipeline tracking shows no new SOPs completed in 2+ weeks | Reassess priority. Determine blocker: admin capacity? Chris review bottleneck? Unclear requirements? |

### Pastoral Safeguards Are Weakening

| Signal | How to Detect | Corrective Action |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Prayer requests not being properly tiered | Monthly routing review shows Tier 2-3 items routed as Tier 1 | Retrain on tier definitions. Review specific examples. Increase review frequency |
| Sensitive information shared inappropriately | Staff or congregant reports information breach | Immediate investigation. Determine scope. Retrain. If severe: consider access restriction or personnel action |
| Background check results visible to wrong people | Access audit shows unauthorized viewers | Immediately restrict access. Review storage and permission settings. Retrain |
| Benevolence decisions made without proper process | Exception log shows benevolence approvals outside normal flow | Enforce the process. No exceptions to separation of duties. If process is too slow for emergencies: create an emergency sub-process with Chris approval |
| Admin representing Chris's voice without calibration | Staff or external contacts receive communications that do not match Chris's tone or intent | Pull back correspondence delegation level to L2-L3. Increase review cadence. Recalibrate templates and guidelines |

## Quick-Start Summary: What to Do Monday

1. **Print the Escalation Quick Reference** (Tool 4). Give one to each admin.
2. **Set financial thresholds** if not already set: purchase ($500), donor ($5,000), budget anomaly (5%/$1,000).
3. **Sign confidentiality agreements** with both admins.
4. **Set up the task tracker** (spreadsheet with columns from the revised matrix).
5. **Schedule recurring calendar items:** weekly 1-on-1 with each admin (30 min), weekly team huddle (15 min).
6. **Hand off the first 12 tasks per admin** from the Week 1-2 list (Section G, Phase 1).
7. **Write the first 3 SOPs** (or delegate drafting at L2): Inbox Triage, Prayer Request Routing, Calendar Management.
8. **Start the reverse-delegation self-audit** (Tool 5) on Friday of Week 1.
9. **Brief ministry owners** at next staff meeting: overview of the framework, what is coming, what they will own.
10. **Block 30 minutes on Friday afternoon** for the weekly delegation health review (Section I, Friday rhythm).

This is a management system, not a document. It works only if it is used, reviewed, and adjusted weekly. The 90-day plan is a starting point. The review gates are where the real decisions happen.
