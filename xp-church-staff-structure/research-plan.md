# Research Plan: Church Staff Structure at 1200+ Attendance

## Primary Question
What is the optimal staff structure for a church with 1200+ weekly attendance, with particular focus on how responsibilities are divided between the Lead Pastor and Executive Pastor?

## Decision Context
- **Who:** Chris Dewar, Executive Pastor / COO at Mosaic Wadsworth (growing church, Northeast Ohio)
- **Decision:** How to structure the staff org chart as Mosaic grows toward and beyond 1200 weekly attendance
- **Key tension:** Ensuring the LP/XP role division is clear, sustainable, and positioned for continued growth
- **Time horizon:** Current planning through next 3-5 years of growth

## Sub-Questions
1. What are the common staffing models used by churches with 1200+ attendance?
2. What is the typical staff-to-attendance ratio at this size, and how does it break down by role type (ministry vs. operations)?
3. How do churches at this size divide responsibilities between Lead Pastor and Executive Pastor?
4. What ministry areas typically have dedicated staff at this size?
5. What are common pitfalls in church staff structure as organizations scale past 1000?
6. What does research say about optimal spans of control in church settings?
7. How do high-performing large churches handle the tension between ministry leadership and operations leadership?

## Scope Boundaries
- **In scope:** Churches 1200-5000 attendance (large church category); Protestant/evangelical tradition; US context; staffing ratios; LP/XP role division; org chart patterns; growth-stage considerations
- **Out of scope:** Megachurch structures (10,000+); Catholic/Orthodox polity; international contexts; volunteer structure (except where it intersects paid staff); detailed compensation data
- **Time range:** Research from 2015-2026 preferred; foundational works accepted regardless of date

## What Would Change the Conclusion
If the evidence shows that the LP/XP model itself is less important than the *clarity of role definition* regardless of model — i.e., that multiple models work equally well as long as boundaries are explicit — that would shift the recommendation from "adopt model X" to "adopt any model but define it rigorously."

## Source Priority
1. Church leadership research organizations (Leadership Network, Barna, ECFA, Vanderbloemen, LifeWay Research)
2. Peer-reviewed organizational research applied to churches
3. Published books by experienced church leaders (Malphurs, Rainer, Bonem, Patterson)
4. Case studies from named churches at this size
5. Practitioner blogs and conference content (with appropriate caveats)

## Search Strategy
- **Phase 1 (Orienting):** Broad searches on church staffing ratios, large church org structure
- **Phase 2 (Targeted):** Specific searches on LP/XP role division, executive pastor responsibilities, church staff-to-attendance ratios
- **Phase 3 (Verification):** Search for failure modes, criticisms of common models, churches that restructured
- **Phase 4 (Gap-filling):** Follow up on any weak sub-questions
